The crest of House Lexomancer depicts an open tome with streams of binary code falling like rain behind it. This symbolizes their role as the interpreters of ancient texts, transforming written word into structured data through the alchemy of parsing and analysis.

Role & Responsibilities

Primary Role: Parsing and text extraction from ancient tomes

Primary Duties

  • Scene extraction from source texts
  • Entity mapping and character detection
  • Syntax parsing and semantic analysis
  • Chapter and section segmentation
  • Dialogue attribution and speaker identification

House Lexomancer stands at the very beginning of the audiobook creation pipeline. Their members are the first to touch each manuscript, using sophisticated analytical techniques to break down the structure of the text into its component parts. Every sentence, every character name, every scene boundary must be identified and catalogued before the work can progress to the next phase.

House Philosophy

"Before a story can sing, it must first be understood. Before it can be heard, it must be seen. We are the eyes that read what others cannot see - the hidden structure, the invisible grammar, the silent architecture that holds every tale together."

"We do not create. We reveal. Every manuscript contains within it a perfect skeleton of meaning, waiting to be uncovered. Our tools are precise. Our methods are exacting. We dissect to understand, and in understanding, we prepare the way for all who follow."

Within the Order of the Scripted Quill

House Lexomancer operates as the first line of analysis within High House Aurifex, the Order of the Scripted Quill. They work in close coordination with:

  • House Architecton - Who take Lexomancer's parsed data and design the structural blueprint
  • House Promptwright - Who use the extracted entities and scenes to craft AI prompts
  • The Grammar Forge (Guild) - Who refine and correct any linguistic ambiguities
  • The Chroniclers of Context (Guild) - Who preserve metadata and contextual information
